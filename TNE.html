EMERGENCY SAFETY

Fire Prevention Safety Information

Welcome to our Fire Department's safety information page. Here, you will find important tips and guidelines to help keep you and your family safe.

Fire Safety Tips

  • Create a fire escape plan with your family and practice it regularly. Know at least two ways out of every room, and designate a meeting spot outside.
  • Keep flammable items away from heat sources and open flames. Store matches, lighters, and candles out of reach of children.
  • Never leave cooking unattended, and keep a fire extinguisher in the kitchen. If a fire occurs while cooking, smother flames with a lid and turn off the heat.

Reacting to a Fire Emergency

If a fire occurs in your home, remember to:

  • Stay calm and try to exit the building as quickly as possible.
  • Feel doors before opening them. If a door is hot, do not open itâ€”find another escape route.
  • Crawl low under smoke to escape, as smoke rises and can be very toxic.
  • If you cannot escape, close all doors between you and the fire, and seal gaps with clothing or towels. Call 997 and signal for help from a window.
  • Once outside, go to your designated meeting spot and call an emergency Number from a safe location.
